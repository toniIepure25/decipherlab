# Tentative Abstract

Low-resource historical document workflows rarely want one universal uncertainty policy. Some cases require rescue-preserving candidate retention, while others benefit from compact shortlists under strict review budgets. We therefore frame uncertainty control as an interactive verification problem rather than a single decoder-selection problem. Building on earlier support-aware propagation analysis, we introduce a support-aware interactive verification system for historical document analysis with explicit `rescue-first` and `shortlist-first` operating profiles, explicit review budgets `k=2,3,5`, and explicit preserve / prune / defer behavior.

The system builds a confusion network from grouped image crops, extracts a small auditable set of support features, and uses a lightweight profile selector to recommend the more suitable operating mode for the current support regime and review budget. The selector and the underlying profiles remain interpretable: they use logistic gates and threshold rules over entropy, candidate-set size, support counts, posterior family, fragility flags, and budget indicators, and they log profile choice, decoder choice, beam width, budget, and defer decisions for every example.

We evaluate the system on two real grouped/token-aligned corpora and a redesigned real downstream task using shortlist recall, effort-adjusted utility, grouped top-k rescue, fragile-case utility, and regret to the best profile in hindsight. The selector achieves mean effort-adjusted utility `0.452`, mean grouped top-k delta `0.162` vs fixed, mean recall@budget `0.591`, and mean effort-adjusted regret `0.011` to the best profile. In aggregate, it stays close to `shortlist-first` on effort utility while moving much closer to `rescue-first` on grouped rescue. It routes mostly toward `shortlist-first` in Historical Newspapers `cluster_distance` regimes and mostly toward `rescue-first` in tight-budget ScaDS.AI `cluster_distance` regimes, matching the different support needs of the two corpora. Fragile downstream cases defer under `k=2/3` and recover utility at `k=5`. The selector can even exceed the best single fixed profile in calibrated Historical Newspapers regimes. The result is not a universal best policy and still trails the strongest fixed baseline on average effort-adjusted utility. Its contribution is instead a near-oracle, budget-aware interactive verification component that aligns uncertainty control with real archival review objectives.
