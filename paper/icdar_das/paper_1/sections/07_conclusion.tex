\section{Conclusion}

The strongest evidence-bound conclusion is narrower than a general structured-recovery claim. Preserved uncertainty reliably improves real symbol and grouped alternative retention, and grouped rescue can replicate on real grouped corpora. Downstream propagation remains conditional: it depends on measurable support conditions and often fails even after grouped rescue occurs.

The paper is therefore best understood as a support-aware uncertainty propagation study for low-resource glyph and grouped recognition. Its contribution is not a universal decoder win, but a field-grounded empirical account of when preserved uncertainty remains useful as inference moves from local symbol evidence to grouped and downstream reasoning.
