# Final Convergence Audit

## 1. Scholarly grounding problems

- Page 1-2: the manuscript had almost no field-facing literature positioning beyond dataset references and internal claim framing.
- Page 2: Section II was titled `Scope and Positioning`, but it functioned as a boundary statement rather than a related-work section. That made the paper feel self-contained rather than situated in document analysis, HTR, calibration, or structured decoding work.
- References page: the active bibliography was almost entirely local dataset placeholders, which made the paper look under-cited even though the empirical content was careful.

## 2. Narrative/section-flow problems

- Page 1: the introduction stated the claim clearly, but it moved from scope to contribution without first establishing the underlying recognition problem in field language.
- Page 3-4: the Results section felt segmented into separate branch updates rather than one argument with a clear arc from replicated grouped rescue to support-gated downstream rescue.
- Page 4: the propagation analysis was strong scientifically, but it appeared after several summary paragraphs and therefore read slightly like an add-on instead of the paper's analytical destination.

## 3. Title/abstract/front-page problems

- Page 1: the title was accurate but generic; it read like a report heading rather than a conference paper title with a memorable question or point of view.
- Page 1: `Affiliation TODO` looked visibly unfinished.
- Page 1: the abstract was solid, but it still sounded a bit like a status summary of branch contents rather than a direct conference-paper argument.

## 4. Table-design problems

- Page 3: Table I tried to carry the whole evidence ladder in sentence-like prose. The rightmost column was especially text-heavy, which made the table look like a compressed project summary rather than a selective paper table.
- Appendix tables were readable, but the hierarchy between the main-paper table and appendix tables was not sharp enough.

## 5. Figure-design problems

- Page 3: the grouped-replication figure showed the correct signal, but it behaved like a diagnostics panel. Abbreviated labels such as `Hist. / Cal.` and `ScaDS / Dist.` required extra decoding.
- Page 5: the propagation figure carried the analytical contribution, but its placement and label economy made it feel disconnected from the replicated grouped result that motivates it.
- Across pages 3-5: the two main figures looked like separate dashboard exports instead of one coherent visual story.

## 6. Float/layout/page-balance problems

- Page 3: Table I and the grouped-replication figure sat close enough in the flow that the page felt evidence-dense and slightly cramped.
- Page 5: the propagation figure landed after the main argument had already been stated in prose, so the page balance emphasized summary before explanation.
- Appendix pages: the appendix still looked somewhat like a holding area for leftover artifacts rather than a designed supplement.

## 7. Appendix/support-material problems

- Appendix opening text described the appendix's purpose clearly, but the material still read like support exports instead of a tightly curated trust-and-robustness supplement.
- The downstream redesign figure was useful, but the appendix lacked a stronger sentence explaining why it stayed in the appendix while the main figure should carry the paper's core story.

## 8. Tone/prose problems

- The manuscript was careful, but several paragraphs still sounded like internal branch bookkeeping: `the branch`, `the paper combines four evidence layers`, and repeated reminders about what the paper is not.
- Several sections repeated the same limitation boundary in slightly different words, which made the tone more report-like than necessary.
- The Results section often reported values first and argument second; a more conference-polished version should foreground the conclusion and use the numbers as support.
