\section{Conclusion}

The strongest evidence-bound conclusion is narrower than a general structured-recovery claim. Preserved uncertainty reliably improves real symbol and grouped alternative retention. Grouped rescue can replicate on real grouped corpora. Downstream propagation depends on measurable support conditions and often fails even after grouped rescue occurs.

This makes the paper best understood as a support-aware uncertainty propagation study for low-resource glyph and grouped sequence recognition. Its contribution is not a universal decoder win. It is a field-grounded empirical account of when preserved uncertainty remains useful as inference moves from local symbol evidence to grouped and downstream reasoning.
